# Getting Started with AURA Protocol

This guide will walk you through the essential steps to begin interacting with the AURA Protocol, from setting up your environment to connecting your wallet and making your first deposit.

## Prerequisites

Before you start, ensure you have the following:

1.  **EVM-Compatible Wallet:** A cryptocurrency wallet that supports EVM-compatible networks (e.g., MetaMask, WalletConnect, Coinbase Wallet).
2.  **Stablecoins:** Sufficient stablecoins (e.g., USDC, DAI, USDT) in your connected wallet on the target network (e.g., Sepolia Testnet or Ethereum Mainnet).
3.  **Network Funds (Gas):** A small amount of the native currency of the target network (e.g., ETH on Sepolia/Mainnet) to cover transaction fees (gas).

## Connecting Your Wallet

The AURA Protocol frontend requires you to connect your wallet to interact with the smart contracts.

1.  **Access the Application:** Open the AURA Protocol frontend application in your web browser.
2.  **Click "Connect Wallet":** Locate and click the "Connect Wallet" button, usually found in the top-right corner of the dashboard.
3.  **Select Your Wallet:** A prompt will appear, asking you to choose your preferred wallet provider (e.g., MetaMask).
4.  **Approve Connection:** Follow the instructions in your wallet to approve the connection to the AURA Protocol. Once connected, your wallet address will be displayed.

## Navigating the Dashboard

Upon connecting your wallet, you will be directed to the main dashboard, which provides an overview of the vault's performance and your personal position.

-   **Vault Statistics:** Displays key metrics like Total Value Locked (TVL), Vault APY (Annual Percentage Yield), the current strategy being employed, and total profit generated.
-   **Deposit / Withdraw Tabs:** This is where you can manage your stablecoin deposits into and withdrawals from the AURA Vault.
-   **Strategy Overview:** Provides detailed information about the underlying AI-powered arbitrage strategy.

Continue to the [Frontend Usage](/frontend-usage) section for more detailed instructions on how to deposit and withdraw funds.
